Glossary

Acceleration
The rate of change of velocity of an object.
Ampere
A unit of electric current in the metric system.
Antimatter
Material composed of the antiparticle to the corresponding particles of ordinary matter.
Applied Physics
The application of physics to solve technical or practical problems.
Black Hole
A region of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing, no particles or radiation, can escape.
Capacitor
A device used to store an electric charge, consisting of one or more pairs of conductors separated by an insulator.
Crest
The highest point or peak of a wave.
Diffraction
The bending of waves around obstacles and openings.
Doppler Effect
The change in frequency of a wave in relation to an observer who is moving relative to the wave source.
Electromagnetism
The study of the electromagnetic force that occurs between electrically charged particles.
Electron
A stable subatomic particle with a charge of negative electricity.
Entropy
A thermodynamic quantity representing the unavailability of a system's thermal energy for conversion into mechanical work.
Frequency
The rate at which a vibration occurs that constitutes a wave, in a material or in an electromagnetic field, usually measured per second.
Gravity
The force that attracts a body towards the center of the earth, or towards any other physical body having mass.
Hydraulics
The science concerned with the laws governing water or other liquids in motion and their applications in engineering.
Inertia
The resistance of any physical object to any change in its state of motion, including changes to its speed and direction.
Kinetic Energy
The energy that an object possesses due to its motion.
Laser
A device that emits light through a process of optical amplification based on the stimulated emission of electromagnetic radiation.
Magnetism
A physical phenomenon produced by the motion of electric charge, resulting in attractive and repulsive forces between objects.
Nanotechnology
The manipulation and manufacture of materials and devices on the scale of atoms or small groups of atoms.
Neutrino
A subatomic particle that is very similar to an electron but has no electrical charge and a very small mass.
Ohm
The SI unit of electrical resistance, expressing the resistance in a circuit transmitting a current of one ampere when subjected to a potential difference of one volt.
Photon
A particle representing a quantum of light or other electromagnetic radiation.
Quantum Mechanics
The branch of mechanics that deals with the mathematical description of the motion and interaction of subatomic particles.
Relativity
The dependence of physical phenomena on relative motion of the observer and the observed objects.
Semiconductor
A solid substance that has a conductivity between that of an insulator and most metals.
Superconductor
A substance capable of becoming superconducting at sufficiently low temperatures.
Thermodynamics
The branch of physical science that deals with the relations between heat and other forms of energy.
Trough
The lowest point of a wave.
Wavelength
The distance between successive crests of a wave, especially points in a sound wave or electromagnetic wave.
Waves
Oscillations accompanied by a transfer of energy that travel through space or mass.
Velocity
The speed of something in a given direction.
Force
An influence that causes an object to undergo a change in speed, direction, or shape.
Energy
The property of matter and radiation that is manifest as a capacity to perform work.
Mass
A measure of the amount of matter in an object, typically in kilograms or grams.
